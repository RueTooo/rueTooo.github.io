Aerendyl 12

Session 12 Day 22 of Bloomwave(?), year 515 of the Third Age
(Time works differently here. Who knows what date it might really be!)
Before the imminent departure to the Feywilds, we take a moment to pen letters to those who hold a place of significance in our hearts. My heart clenches at the thought of never returning, of succumbing to the unknown, but the risk beckons us boldly. We choose paper birds as our messengers, capable of carrying our last words for up to fifty strides.

To Malachai, I scribe a letter sharing my path ahead—|the Feywilds—and my resolve to find him in the wake of our endeavour's success or failure. And to Aoife, I spill my gratitude for the wisdom she imparted upon me; the teachings that armoured my spirit for what lays ahead.

Caspian, with that pervasive air of mystery clouding him, transforms the passage, opening a doorway to Mosskarm, our gateway to the other side. The space we step into bears significant resemblance to our departure point but embellished with a grander tree and an overall tidier presence. A Caspian lookalike – Calahan – awaits us there, ready to reveal secrets etched into the walls at our questioning.

Their projections illustrate most of the Winter Court, with fiery spheres that burst, magma flowing, and two moons behind the Mosskarm peaks, one steadfast, one ever moving.

A myriad of plans sprout amidst us—gathering supplies, gathering whispers—while Corvaux advises caution, insisting on aliases to mask our true selves in this beguiling realm. And so the masks we don, adopting names not our own:

  • Aeren morphs into Kohatu.
  • Mirri transforms to Mirabelle.
  • Thaddeus cloaks himself as Ted Barney, with a third guise of Dath Lliuq secreted away.
  • Runa reverses her name to Anur.
  • And Corvaux reforges her name into Varus.

I offer the warmth of my amber orb that Vigil gifted me to Mirabelle, sharing the flickering warmth as I hoist her upon my back.

We seek snow outside, only to be met with humid air, a landscape of rock, sunflowers embracing a river of magma—an underground city chiselled into the heart of a volcano, where steam and heat power machinations foreign yet intriguing. It's a steampunk rendition of the home I recall, complete with a bustling populace weaving through steam-absorbing verdure.

Corvaux's touch upon a sunflower releases a yellow powder—sulfur, not the Pale Blight we feared. Apologies lodge in my throat as relief floods me; we are spared the encounter with that scourge.

Our steps lead us to an ink seller named Pride, who directs us to the other needs of our voyage: Crimson Inn, and a nearby hunter’s stall. Mirabelle secures me a luxurious cloak from the hunter for some coin, for which I utter thanks in Sylvan, much to their undisguised delight.

At Flaming Fortune, we might procure other trinkets of warmth, but likely not without draining our purses dry. Ted's act of learning Sylvan that ensues is nothing short of a marvel; Pride imbues him with knowledge of the language through some type of arcane … ritual? Either way: I find myself inspired by it, but, admittedly, a touch envious as well.

We head to Crimson Inn at last with Ted’s new knowledge in tow. A stabby knife contest of some sort ensues at a table behind ours. This inn promises an odd form of camaraderie, and I note the genasi across the way—a silent challenge flickers between us.

As Ted delves deeper into the contest, though, I cannot pass up the invitation to join ( to help further Ted's pursuit of finding out more about Zirel ). Mirrabelle and I both lending our hands to the knives. Such games may veil more than meets the eye, transactions of information resting behind every throw.

With Runa and Corvaux by our sides at the table, I can now only hope I won’t lose yet another finger.
