Aerendyl 26

Day 28/29 of Bloomweave, 515 T.A.?
In the quiet hours before we sought out our bedrolls, I reached out to Wisp with the sending stone, discussing the peculiar sound haunting my thoughts. Wisp confirmed it was related to the star she could hear, and, since the Crimson Gaze is departing in two days, asked if I could trace the sound to its source before then—two days! How am I to even do that? It lingers as an unsettling challenge even now—cross-planar communication holds a sliver of uncertainty, after all, and I don’t expect the Crimson Gaze to hang around in the Feywild after departing.

The next morning, I got to wondering whether something's wrong with Corvaux—not in the offensive sense, but she's been sleeping; dreaming, even, instead of going into a trance. I wonder to what end she's doing that, but I haven't the heart to pry.

Instead, I asked Mirri not to indulge further in theft while she pored through a massive tome, urging some respect toward Deidre's home. Yet as words faltered, I found Mirri unmoved—and, somewhat comically, the egg she pilfered missing from her possession. I got the impression she blamed me for it …

Oh, well. Maybe Grout or Ralph got their sticky little paws on it. In any case, Corvaux and I headed off to prepare tea for the group atop glowing coals, which is when we stumbled upon symbols amongst the wood—a death's-head moth and a rough diamond, the latter which was especially familiar to me as a symbol of Dounuma's (or Dumathoin, if you will). A conversation with Deidre about such ominous icons is due at some point, but we had more pressing matters to resolve, first.

Together, Corvaux and I sought Deidre, where I clumsily offered tea, only to witness it discarded with nonchalance—an embarrassing testament to our different tastes, I suppose. We apologised for Mirri's pilfering. In retrospect, I'm relieved she wasn't there to hear it. I fear she'd have struck us both upside the head with a well-aimed Eldritch Blast in retaliation for speaking on her behalf.

Before long, our ensemble convened to devise a plan. The vault beneath the manor most likely contained the heart of the curse. Deidre recounted a symbol to beware of—a symbol eerily familiar the several I've found in the Stonecutter's journal.

decided to reveal the contents of the journal to the group—not just one person; not just to Corvaux as I did before, but everyone. The depth of the Stonecutter's work startled both Mirri and me with its potency, detailing entities known for pilfering souls, evoking the name of the dreaded Orcus, and a record narrating an inquiry into the lineage and purpose of missing souls. The symbols somehow seemed to depict anchors of such souls, in a sense?

Mirri stressed the power described in just these few pages, warning that it must never fall into Tlanbourn's hands. In fact, she would sooner set the journal and myself on fire than let that happen. She would have to; my resolve to hang onto these wretched pages and follow them to my destination is stronger now than ever before. I'll not give this up willingly.

Based on these findings, though, I theorized the circular symbols Deidre sketched out might just be what's anchoring her here—her soul, specifically. Depending on the exact state of the symbol, we might need to destroy it, or dismantle it by tampering with the Weave that powers it. It'll be tricky nonetheless, I expect.

So we traversed to the basement, Ralph and Grout left above to avoid harm. We found the entry sealed, but with Corvaux's touch, the stone slid away, revealing the steps into depths gilded as solid gold. Below, statues and a white bowl before a lit effigy of Lord Aurelius (or Eristalon, as we've come to know the name) beckoned for an offering. Deidre's wooden wolf idol granted us passage.

In the next chamber, a grand harp stood amid silence, and we discovered a sigil requiring a key—the solution lay within a treasury that awoke armoured assailants as Corvaux and Mirri crossed its threshold. After a scuffle, we claimed the gold and gems inside, aquamarine crystals particularly catching my eye—a near sacred piece of my homeland.

Mirri's critique of my fascination with the gems sparked bitterness; I felt the chasm my earlier request might have given way to, widen. Nonetheless, we affirmed the spoils were not Deidre's to start with (so I assume they belonged to this Eristalon), and divided them fairly amongst all of us. In hindsight, I wonder what might become of us if he ever finds out what we took from his treasury …

With this chamber practically emptied, a secret chamber revealed itself, one that turned our stomachs with decay and one that encompassed something other than golden, shiny wealth—organs, daggers, and the lingering questions that now we must still answer.
