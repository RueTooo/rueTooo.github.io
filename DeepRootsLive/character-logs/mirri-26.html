Right. Eating my words. Aeren shared this Stonecutter's journal as Deirdre sketched out the sigils, or an approximation of them, that keep her bound to this place. Whoever this Stonecutter is, their work is fearsome to behold—interrogating demons, devils, and hags? There is even mention of Orcus. Some of this is pre-spell plague. Gods, I am shaking with fear. He can never be allowed to get his hands on this. I like Aerendyl, however irritating he is at times, but if it comes to it… Over and over, this journal details one thing: souls are the purest and most concentrated power in the universe… and he has tried to take mine. He failed once.

If he knows the truth—he will not fail again.
I am trying to swallow past my own heart lodged in my throat and focus on the task at hand. To undo such an enchantment or curse requires one of two things: delicate handiwork or brute strength. Thankfully, I have the former in spades.
We’ve headed downstairs. Corvaux is working on the door that leads below, where whatever it is that holds Deidre here is located and—
What the fuck do you mean Deidre has no pulse?

Gods. There is no pulse at all. She is as cold as death. What the fuck is going on here?

I will not let Deirdre out of my sight until I know what is really going on here. My companions are so quick to trust, even Luck, who I thought would know better. We have no idea who she is, nor what waits for us below. This place belonged to Eristalon himself, if the gaudy statues are any indication. And yet, these fools I travel with are turning a blind eye. I know I am guilty of falling for whatever honeyed words drop from a pretty face, but this is too much, even for a tried-and-true fool like myself. No pulse, blamed on the “curse.” Telling us absolutely nothing about what we are facing. Someone is going to get hurt.

The only good thing that has come from this is that we are all now 2,000 gold richer… and Aeren was bloodied by a living suit of armor. Was it petty to chastise him like a child, as he did to me, though I am more than twice his age? Yes. Do I feel bad about it? Not in the slightest. But we will talk about that another time, when I am not watching Deidre like a hawk.

We have left the treasure room behind and come to a tomb. The smell is unbearable, though only Corvaux lost her breakfast. Two of the coffins were open, but one is empty. Much as I want to investigate closer, Deirdre has stepped into the next room and I intend to follow. Whatever she’s looking for, I need to get my hands on anyway if she has any hope of having this undone.
